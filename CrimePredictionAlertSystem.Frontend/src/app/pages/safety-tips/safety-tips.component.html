Personal Safety

Stay aware of your surroundings avoid walking while distracted by your phone or headphones. Avoid poorly lit areas at night and use well-traveled routes. Trust your instincts if something feels off, leave the area immediately. Do not share your location publicly on social media in real time.

Home Safety

Lock all doors and windows when leaving your home, even for a short time. Install motion-sensor lights around entrances and dark corners. Don’t leave spare keys under doormats or flowerpots — give them to someone you trust. Use smart doorbell cameras and security systems if possible.

Vehicle Safety

Always lock your vehicle, even when parked at home. Do not leave valuables in plain sight — place them in the trunk. Park in well-lit, secure areas, especially at night. Check your back seat before getting into your car.

Online Safety

Never share personal information with unknown contacts online. Use strong, unique passwords for your accounts and update them regularly. Watch out for phishing emails or fake job/investment scams. Enable two-factor authentication on all important accounts.
